Welcome to Nina Hosseini-Kivanani's Research Page

I am a researcher at the University of Luxembourg, where I focus on advancing natural language processing (NLP), computer vision, and speech technologies for linguistically diverse communities. My research lies at the intersection of phonetics, acoustic analysis, speech processing, and automatic speech recognition, with a particular emphasis on developing solutions for underrepresented languages.

Currently, I lead the LuxVoice project, a groundbreaking initiative to create the first emotionally expressive Text-to-Speech system for Luxembourgish. This project combines advanced AI with cultural sensitivity to preserve and promote this unique language in the digital age. I am passionate about building speech technology that sounds natural, expressive, and authentic to the communities it serves.

My academic background spans computational linguistics and cognitive science, with expertise in sentiment analysis, multilingual speech recognition, and acoustic analysis. I have worked with multiple languages across different language families, and I am particularly interested in studying cognitive functions through a computational lens. My recent work includes breakthrough research on Alzheimer's disease screening using drawing analysis and cross-language intelligibility analysis for Parkinson's disease patients.

Research Vision

My research is driven by the belief that every language deserves access to modern speech and language technology. This motivates my work in:

  • Speech Technology for Low-Resource Languages: Developing innovative approaches to create high-quality speech synthesis and recognition systems for languages with limited digital resources, from acoustic modeling to expressive TTS
  • Multilingual & Cross-lingual Analysis: Creating practical tools that work across languages, enabling comparative studies and universal insights into speech patterns and linguistic phenomena
  • AI for Healthcare: Applying speech and drawing analysis to medical diagnostics, particularly for neurodegenerative diseases like Alzheimer's and Parkinson's
  • Community-Centered Development: Working directly with language communities to ensure technologies meet real-world needs and preserve cultural authenticity

Languages are not merely data points in my research—they represent living communities, cultural heritage, and human expression. I've had the privilege of working with multiple languages and am committed to open-source development. Do you need help developing speech or language technology for your language? Let me know!

Beyond research, I'm passionate about nature, mountains, and music. I enjoy playing the harmonica and kalimba, and I love learning new languages (currently studying Italian and French). I believe these interests enrich my perspective on language, sound, and human communication.

LuxVoice Project

LuxVoice is a groundbreaking speech technology project focused on the Luxembourgish language. Through advanced machine learning and linguistic analysis, we're creating the first comprehensive speech synthesis and recognition system for Luxembourgish.

Our team is dedicated to preserving and promoting this unique language through cutting-edge speech technology, ensuring it remains vibrant in our increasingly digital world.

GLORIA Project

GLORIA (Global Research on Intelligent Acoustic Analysis) represents a breakthrough initiative in computational acoustics, combining advanced signal processing with artificial intelligence to revolutionize sound analysis.

Our interdisciplinary team works across continents to develop acoustic monitoring solutions for biodiversity conservation, urban planning, and environmental protection.
